## SECTION 1 — PURPOSE, SCOPE, AND BACKGROUND
## 1.1  Purpose
This Occupational Health and Safety Compliance Policy Manual (hereinafter "the Manual") establishes the
mandatory behavioral standards governing all activities conducted within the production facilities of
Kafaoglu Metal Plastik Makine San. ve Tic. A.S. (hereinafter "the Company"). The Manual defines which
observable behaviors on the production floor are classified as safe or unsafe, and describes the monitoring
and alert mechanisms in place to detect and respond to unsafe behaviors in real time.

Unsafe behaviors are a leading cause of occupational accidents. Globally, it is estimated that approximately
2.3 million occupational accidents and diseases occur each year, resulting in around 0.3 million deaths. The
economic costs of work-related injuries and illnesses are estimated at between 1.8% and 6.0% of GDP
across countries, with an average of 4% according to the International Labour Organization (ILO). The
Company is committed to minimizing the contribution of unsafe behavior to these figures within its own
operations.

Despite regular safety inspections and ongoing preventive measures, accidents continue to occur as a
result of breaches of occupational health and safety protocols. Human error cannot be completely
eliminated through inspection alone. This Manual therefore sets out the behavioral standards and
automated monitoring measures that complement inspection activities.

1.2  Scope of Application
This Manual applies to all personnel operating on the production floor, including full-time and temporary
employees, subcontractors, maintenance personnel, and visitors. It defines four specific behavioral domains
— pedestrian movement, equipment intervention, electrical panel management, and forklift load
management — within which safe and unsafe behaviors have been identified in consultation with the
Company's occupational safety expert.

1.3  Monitoring and Enforcement
The Company has installed an automated real-time behavior detection and warning system on the
production floor. This system continuously monitors live video feeds from IP cameras installed throughout
the facility. When an unsafe behavior is detected, the system immediately issues both audible and visual
warnings in the area where the behavior is occurring, and automatically logs the event for reporting. The
provisions of this Manual define the behaviors that trigger these automated responses.

## SECTION 2 — DEFINITIONS AND CLASSIFICATION OF BEHAVIORS
## 2.1  Behavioral Classification Framework
All observable behaviors on the production floor are classified into two categories: Safe Behaviors and
Unsafe Behaviors. For each of the four operational domains covered by this Manual, a specific safe
behavior and a specific unsafe behavior have been defined. These definitions form the basis for the
automated monitoring system's detection logic and for the alert and reporting procedures described in
## Section 7.

## SECTION 3 — PEDESTRIAN WALKWAY REGULATIONS
## 3.1  Hazard Context
The production floor involves the simultaneous presence of operating machinery, moving forklifts, and on-
foot personnel. Separating pedestrian movement from machinery and vehicle activity is a fundamental
safety measure. The Designated Safe Walkway provides a defined, physically marked route for all foot
traffic on the production floor.
## 3.2  Walkway Markings
The Designated Safe Walkway is physically marked on the production floor using green painted lines.
These green markings define the boundary of the permitted pedestrian zone. The floor of the facility also
features yellow painted lines that further delineate zones within the workspace. Personnel on foot must
remain within the green-marked pathway boundaries.

## NOTE
The green-marked Designated Safe Walkway boundaries are visible in the facility's
surveillance camera views and form the primary reference boundary for the automated
walkway compliance detection system.

## 3.3  Behavioral Standards
3.3.1  Required Behavior — Safe Walkway (Compliant)
Safe Walkway is defined as movement along the production floor that remains within the boundaries of the
green-marked Designated Safe Walkway. This is the required and expected behavior for all personnel
moving through the production facility on foot.

3.3.2  Non-Compliant Behavior — Safe Walkway Violation
A Safe Walkway Violation is defined as movement or presence outside the boundaries of the green-marked
Designated Safe Walkway. This is the most frequently occurring unsafe behavior documented at this
facility. A Safe Walkway Violation is detected when a person's position in the video frame places them
beyond the green walkway boundaries and into an area designated for machinery or vehicle operation.

## WARNING
The Safe Walkway Violation is the highest-frequency unsafe behavior recorded at this
facility. The frequency of this behavior reflects a tendency among personnel to treat minor
deviations from the walkway as inconsequential. Any deviation from the green-marked
boundaries places the individual in proximity to forklift and machinery hazards and will be
treated as an unsafe behavior event requiring immediate response.

## SECTION 4 — EQUIPMENT AND MACHINERY INTERVENTION
## 4.1  Hazard Context
Interaction with production equipment and machinery by personnel who are not cleared for the relevant task
creates risk of injury to the intervening individual and to others in the area, and may disrupt production
processes. Authorized personnel are visually distinguished from unauthorized personnel by the safety vest
they wear. This vest-based visual identification system is the primary mechanism by which intervention
compliance is assessed and monitored.

## 4.2  Vest Identification System
The facility operates a safety vest color system to indicate the authorization status of personnel interacting
with equipment:
- Green safety vest: worn by personnel who have been authorized to perform an intervention or
maintenance activity at a specific piece of equipment. The green vest is the observable indicator of
authorization.
- Red-black safety vest: worn by general production personnel who are not cleared for direct
equipment intervention. This is the standard vest for production floor personnel not performing an
authorized task.

The color of the safety vest worn by a person interacting with equipment is the primary observable criterion
for classifying the behavior as Authorized or Unauthorized Intervention.

## 4.3  Behavioral Standards
4.3.1  Required Behavior — Authorized Intervention (Compliant)
An Authorized Intervention is defined as an interaction with or working on production equipment performed
by a person who is wearing the designated green safety vest and the required safety equipment for the
task. The green vest indicates that the individual has been cleared to perform the activity in question. This is
the required and expected behavior for all equipment interaction on the production floor.

4.3.2  Non-Compliant Behavior — Unauthorized Intervention
An Unauthorized Intervention is defined as any of the following observable situations:
- A person interacting with or adjusting production equipment while wearing a red-black vest or any
vest other than the designated green authorization vest.
- A person interacting with or adjusting production equipment while not wearing the required safety
equipment for the task.
- A person intervening in a work situation at a production site without the observable indicators of
authorization (i.e., the green vest).

## CRITICAL
## SAFETY
## NOTICE
The green safety vest is the primary and most readily observable indicator of authorization
status. Any person seen interacting with equipment who is not wearing the green vest
must be assumed to be performing an Unauthorized Intervention. This classification
applies regardless of the person's stated intent or role, since the vest system exists
precisely to make authorization status unambiguous to observers and to the automated
monitoring system.

## SECTION 5 — ELECTRICAL PANEL COVER MANAGEMENT
## 5.1  Hazard Context
Electrical panels connected to production machinery are present throughout the facility. The covers of these
panels serve as a protective barrier between personnel and the electrical components of the connected
equipment. Leaving panel covers open or forgetting to close them after accessing the panel is an identified
unsafe behavior at this facility.

## 5.2  Behavioral Standards
5.2.1  Required Behavior — Closed Panel Cover (Compliant)
Closed Panel Cover is defined as the condition in which the cover of an electrical panel connected to a
production machine is fully closed. This is the required and expected state for all electrical panels during
production operations.

5.2.2  Non-Compliant Condition — Opened Panel Cover (Unsafe)
Opened Panel Cover is defined as the condition in which the cover of an electrical panel connected to a
production machine has been left in the open position. This includes both the deliberate leaving of a panel
cover open and the inadvertent forgetting to close a panel cover after accessing it.

An Opened Panel Cover is detected when the surveillance system observes a panel in the open-cover state
during production operations. The condition is an unsafe behavior event regardless of how long the panel
has been open or whether personnel are in the immediate vicinity at the moment of detection.

## WARNING
Leaving a panel cover open — even when doing so feels like a minor or temporary
oversight — is classified as an unsafe behavior under this Manual. The automated
detection system monitors panel cover status continuously and will generate an alert
whenever an open-cover condition is observed.

## SECTION 6 — FORKLIFT LOAD MANAGEMENT
## 6.1  Hazard Context
Forklifts operate within the production floor to carry materials between workstations. Carrying loads that
exceed the defined safe limit creates risks associated with vehicle instability. The safe carrying limit for this
facility is defined in terms of the number of standardized block units carried in a single forklift load operation.

## 6.2  Load Capacity Definition
For all forklift operations within this facility involving standardized block materials, the following load
thresholds apply:

Load Configuration Number of Blocks Carried Compliance Status
Safe Carrying 2 blocks or fewer COMPLIANT — Required
behavior
Carrying Overload with Forklift 3 blocks or more NON-COMPLIANT — Unsafe

These thresholds apply to the standardized blocks used in this facility's production workflow. The number of
blocks visible on the forklift forks during travel is the observable criterion used by the automated detection
system to classify the load as safe or overloaded.

## 6.3  Behavioral Standards
6.3.1  Required Behavior — Safe Carrying (Compliant)
Safe Carrying is defined as operating a forklift while carrying two (2) or fewer standardized blocks in a
single load. This is the required and expected behavior for all forklift load operations within the facility.

6.3.2  Non-Compliant Behavior — Carrying Overload with Forklift
Carrying Overload with Forklift is defined as operating a forklift while carrying three (3) or more
standardized blocks in a single load. Three or more blocks on the forks during any phase of a load
operation — including travel, maneuvering, and loading/unloading — constitutes an unsafe behavior event.

## CRITICAL
## SAFETY
## NOTICE
The block count threshold is unambiguous: two blocks or fewer is safe; three blocks or
more is an overload. Any observed forklift load of three or more standardized blocks will
be treated as a Carrying Overload with Forklift event and will trigger an immediate alert.

## SECTION 7 — SURVEILLANCE, MONITORING, AND ALERT SYSTEM
## 7.1  Camera Infrastructure
The production floor is monitored by two IP cameras (brand: UNV, model: IPC2122CR3-PF40-A) providing
continuous Full HD (1920×1080) video coverage of the active production zones. The cameras are
positioned to provide views of the areas relevant to the four behavioral domains defined in this Manual.
Video feeds are transmitted in real time to the facility's monitoring infrastructure.

Employees working in the monitored areas have been informed of the surveillance system and a consent
form for the protection and use of personal data has been obtained from all personnel whose images are
captured in the recorded footage.

## 7.2  Automated Behavior Detection
An automated unsafe behavior detection system continuously processes the live camera feeds. This
system is capable of identifying the following unsafe behavior conditions in real time:
- Safe Walkway Violation — a person moving or present outside the green-marked walkway
boundaries (Section 3).
- Unauthorized Intervention — a person interacting with production equipment without wearing the
green authorization vest, or without required safety equipment (Section 4).
- Opened Panel Cover — an electrical panel connected to a production machine observed in the open-
cover state during production operations (Section 5).
- Carrying Overload with Forklift — a forklift observed carrying three or more standardized blocks in a
single load (Section 6).
7.3  Monitoring Interface and Reporting
The detection and alert system is managed through a dedicated graphical user interface (GUI) installed at
the facility. This interface provides the following capabilities:
- Live view of the camera feeds covering the production floor.
- Access to the automated event reports generated when unsafe behaviors are detected. Reports are
produced in text document format and record the event type, zone, and timestamp for each detection.

The event reports generated by the system constitute the primary compliance record for the facility. These
reports document each detected unsafe behavior event and are used by the occupational safety expert to
assess behavioral trends and the effectiveness of corrective measures over time.
